About Me



Research interest:


I study earthquake-related ground deformation using computer simulation, space geodesy, and experimental rock physics. For the past years, I extensively worked on investigating postseismic deformation of northeastern Japan following the Mw9.0 2011 Tohoku-oki earthquake. I explored the heterogeneous crust-mantle rheology beneath northeastern Japan using GNSS observations. Using the knowledge of heterogeneous rheology, I build prediction models for postseismic ground displacements after the megathrust earthquake.

Keywords: Crustal deformation, Space Geodesy & Geodynamics, Rock rheology.
